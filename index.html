About Me

I began my career in a clinical pharmacy setting which provided IV, compounding, respiratory, and oral therapies for 200 long-term care and hospice facilities on the east coast of the United States. This experience as a pharmacy technician sparked a lifelong interest in medical technology and pharmacology. Next, I earned my BA in Classics and an MAT in Latin from UNC-Chapel Hill. I spent over a decade in the classroom as a Latin teacher, helping middle schoolers get a jumpstart into high-school level Latin, Roman History, and Mythology. During this time, I started to explore the use of technology in the classroom and beyond.

When I was a project manager working in the construction field, my love affair with technology grew. I discovered a multitude of tools that increased my efficiency, streamlined operations, and increased collaboration, but I also started thinking about growing my skill set and perhaps learning to build tech tools of my own. During this time, I started taking Web Development classes at Middle Tennessee State University. After getting my feet wet, I decided pursue a Web Software Developer Certificate from Nashville Software School.

In my current position at Visualize Health, I specialize in application feature development, research study measure development, and Merit-Based Incentive measure development using C#, JavaScript, D3.js, .NET, ASP.NET, T-SQL, and MS SQL Server. In my role as the CMS MIPS Program Specialist, my efforts are focused on MIPS client coaching, measure documentation research, and compliance consulting. To learn about my adventures as an award-winning public middle-school Latin teacher and how I became an intrepid voyager into the wilds of CMS documentation, check out my LinkedIn page.

Software Development

Humility, a hunger to learn, a willingness to work hard, and flexibility are all characteristics I gleaned from over a decade as a Latin teacher and half a decade as a project manager in residential construction. NSS tempered and sharpened those positive aspects of my personality and taught me how to bring out the best in my teammates as well. My current role provides a tremendous opportunity to grow each day and know that I'm helping patients get the care they need. I relish the idea of learning new technology stacks over the course of my career to become a true polyglot. A few years ago, I began this journey with a basic Web Development class at MTSU out of pure curiosity, and I found my new vocation. I look forward to the intensive planning sessions, complex problems to solve, teamwork, and triumphs each day.

Portfolio

Wise Owl Latin

Built with Angular, JavaScript, Bootstrap 3, and Firebase, this educational video application first allows users to sign in via Google Authentication. A new user can then change his or her avatar and username, which are persisted in Firebase. On click of learning topics in the side navigation, the application runs calls to the database to return related videos and learning games. Users can add a video to favorites, remove one from favorites, and the application protects against duplications. Click here to see the deployed site.

'dose'

Built with Node.js, Express, PostgreSQL, Sequelize, Pug, and Bootstrap 4, this medication tracking application is a full-stack CRUD app that makes calls to the database and the rxNorm API to allow users to add, update, delete and see a list of their medications. The application protects against duplications of drugs in the database and will actually tell the logged-in user what medication to take and when to take it with HTML5 Speech Synthesis. Click here to go to the deployed site.
